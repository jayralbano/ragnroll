# Keypoints

Source: `2026-03-05 01-43-57 long meeting big change part 2.mp4`

1. **UserPilot is unusable — need AI-native analytics**: Milan proposed using UserPilot data + AI to auto-generate support tickets and feature suggestions from user behavior data. Andrew revealed UserPilot's API is enterprise-only (tens of thousands to upgrade) and even a CSV export costs $3,500, making it useless for AI integration. They're considering PostHog as a replacement. This became the key example of why the platform must be AI-native — if tools can't be tapped into by AI/agents, they'll be canceled or users will leave.

2. **Platform strategy — port backend API, build OpenClaw companion**: Andrew laid out three options: (a) keep building on legacy Laravel and accept obsolescence within a year, (b) full rebuild (possible in weeks but wouldn't build the same thing — hub editor and drag-and-drop are dying), or (c) strategically port out core backend APIs (content library, member directory, contacts, hubs) so AI can talk to the backend. The chosen path is option (c) as step one, followed by building an OpenClaw-style chat/phone companion service as a concierge to the existing app — no massive UI overhaul needed, can move fast, and gradually transition users to new experiences.

3. **Distribution is the moat — short window to act**: Andrew argued that anyone can rebuild membership.io in a week now, but what they don't have is distribution — a decade of built-up audience and marketing. Ideas and code mean nothing without it. The window of opportunity is short: the market is finicky but AI is hot, users want time-saving tools. The team needs to make strategic moves now, porting out what they can and trusting their gut on direction even without all the answers.

4. **Ship daily not biweekly — Adam leads scrums, new roadmap coming**: Scrums continue with Adam leading (same as Amar was doing). The big shift: no more two-week sprints — the goal is "can we ship by tomorrow?" Andrew gave a live example: a community member requested a scheduling feature, and on the new platform that should ship in 30 minutes, designed/developed/deployed. The 9:30 Eastern scrum time may change since it's difficult for both US and overseas team members. A new roadmap for the "big rocks" is coming tomorrow.

5. **AI workflow knowledge-sharing sessions proposed for next week**: Asim proposed a casual meetup early next week (Monday or Tuesday) where team members share AI/Claude Code workflows — what worked and what was a disaster. Alkin, Jayr, and Marius already have established workflows. The goal is to speed up knowledge transfer across the whole team (engineers and QA alike), establish shared policies, while recognizing that everyone's workflow will be slightly unique and personal. Regular recurring sessions suggested, not one-off.
